Patient Status

Initial assessment indicates high-priority intervention. Vital signs and reported symptoms suggest acute complications requiring medical supervision.

clinical_notes

Possible Conditions

  • 1

    Pneumonia

    Inflammation of the air sacs in one or both lungs, which may fill with fluid or pus, causing cough with phlegm or pus, fever, chills, and difficulty breathing.

  • 2

    Severe Dehydration

    Critical loss of body fluids often caused by diarrhea or vomiting, characterized by extreme thirst, dark urine, and confusion.

  • 3

    Acute Bronchitis

    Swelling of the bronchial tubes that carry air to your lungs, usually resulting from a viral infection.

  • 4

    Pulmonary Congestion

    Fluid accumulation in the lungs, making it difficult for the patient to perform gas exchange effectively.

  • 5

    Sepsis Risk

    The body's extreme, life-threatening response to an infection. Immediate diagnostic verification is essential.
